Brand identity

In China, it is imperative to have a brand name that resonates with Chinese audiences, that positions you well in the market, and is easy to remember. Choosing a good brand identity shows you are taking your business in China seriously and allows your company to reap the benefits.

Unfortunately, many Chinese translations or transliterations of foreign brand names don’t cut the mustard. They often lack meaning and positive associations, making the company name hard to remember and signalling a lack of commitment to China.

The holy grail of a good Chinese name reflects the sounds of the foreign name using Chinese characters that together have a real, appropriate and positive meaning in Chinese. Benchmark names are Coca-cola (可口可乐), Ikea (宜家) and Carrefour (家乐福) – all of which sound similar to the original, but more importantly sound great in Chinese.

Likewise, brand logos or the corporate visual identity might not be suitable for China. The Chinese characters (or chosen name) needs to be incorporated into the logo, creating a brand identity unique to China while remaining consistent with the visual identity used elsewhere.

Lan-bridge brings our cross-cultural, business and linguistic expertise together to make sure that our clients can benefit from their Chinese brand identity.

Our branding service is tailored to your budget and requirements, but may include the following:
• Workshops and discussions between the client and the linguist
• Proposed names plus rationales
• Dialect homonym checks
• Formal or informal surveys/focus groups
• URL checks
• Trademark checks and registration
• Logo/VI design
• Design of marketing collateral

The brief

It must be short. No more than four characters. It should be pronounced like the international Sveba-Dahlen even in Chinese. It should have a meaning that hints at the brand’s positioning as a leading maker of the highest quality professional ovens and baking equipment. The brands commitment to energy efficiency should be expressed.

The process

We worked with both foreign and local managers over a three day intensive workshop. During the workshop, the problems and requirements were clearly defined and discussed. This collaborative approach allowed us to get a full understanding of Sveba-Dahlen’s brand message, background, competition and future needs. The second day is where the creativity truly began. Our creative team got to work, coming up with a range of proposals and explanations with the client. On the third day, we had a name which we checked with our partners specialising in trademark law.

The result

The name we chose with our client was 斯巴达能. This Chinese name reflects the sound, with 达 meaning achieving and 能 representing Sveba-Dahlen’s energy-saving capabilities. After deciding on the name, we also incorporated the Chinese characters into the existing logo, and designed a range of letterheads, business cards, and templates so the design can be put straight into use. .

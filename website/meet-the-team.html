  1. Michael is the managing director of the whole company. Originally from Hunan, he moved to Chengdu to study engineering and set up the company just after finishing his PhD after he realised the need for better multilingual services for China’s engineering firms. Michael as overseen the company as it has grown from just two staff in Chengdu to an international operation with over 200 team members. He is on the board of the Translators Association of China. Michael is a keen traveller and luckily for him, he doesn’t seem to suffer from jet lag. He is currently studying an MBA with the Cheung Kong Graduate School of Business.

    Maria also studied engineering in Chengdu before joining Lan-bridge in XXX. In China, Maria was in charge of making sure our finances are all in order and now lives in the UK as director of the UK branch where she enjoys discovering and teasing apart the differences between Chinese and UK society, politics and culture.

    Frances lived, studied, worked and travelled in China from 2006 to 2012. She began working at the company’s Chengdu office in 2011 before returning to the UK in 2012, where she helped set up Lan-bridge’s UK office. Fluent in Chinese, Frances is responsible for the day to day running of the office, from dealing with client enquiries, advising clients, managing projects and recruiting translators. Besides spending time pouring over English and Chinese words, she is a big fan of spicy and numbing Sichuan cuisine and so regularly spends time at home trying to recreate those magnificent Chinese feasts for friends and family.

    Laura is our production manager and oversees all translation projects. A true multi-tasking talent, Laura is the person that will do the impossible and make sure that that 10,000 word document you need in a few hours will get to you on time. Laura graduated with a BA from Sichuan International Studies University in 2002 and she has been working at Lan-bridge since 2003, meaning that she knows the company inside out. In her spare time, she enjoys badminton, reading, singing karaoke and playing cards.

    Maggie is in charge of our quality assurance and translator training, as well chipping in with some expert proofreading now and then. Maggie graduated with a BA in Mass Communications from Shanghai International Studies University in 1990 and gained a Translation MA at Sichuan University in 2004 before joining Lan-bridge that same year. She holds a Grade I CATTI (China Accreditation Test for Translators and Interpreters) certificate. Before joining Lan-bridge, she worked in the tourism and banking industries for nearly 10 years. A fast learner, curious and willing to study new things, Maggie is adept at handling documents from a variety of sectors. Apart from her great interest in translation and communication, Maggie enjoys yoga and belly dancing, which help her stay healthy and happy.

    Where would we be in the modern world without computers? And where would we be without Shawn? He’s in charge of the team that set up and runs our project management system, terminology system, websites, online marketing and all the computers and servers. Shawn studied at Northeastern University and before joining Lan-bridge worked at the Beijing headquarters of Advanced Systems Development Co., Ltd. We’d be a technological mess without his skills and solutions, and he’s a friendly and fun team member to boot.
